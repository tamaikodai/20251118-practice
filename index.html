- Evacuation methods by risk level -

This information will serve as a basis for local governments to decide whether to issue a Level 5 Emergency Safety Alert. This is equivalent to Level 5 Alert, which indicates that a disaster has occurred or is imminent. There is an extremely high possibility that some kind of disaster has already occurred. Your life is in imminent danger, so please ensure your safety immediately.


This information serves as a guide for local governments to issue Alert Level 4 evacuation orders. This corresponds to Alert Level 4, which requires evacuation from dangerous areas. In areas where disasters are expected, please pay attention to evacuation orders issued by local governments, and even if evacuation orders have not been issued, please make your own evacuation decisions using information such as Kikikuru (risk distribution) and river water level information.


This is Alert Level 1, which indicates that it is necessary to increase disaster preparedness. Please increase your disaster preparedness by paying attention to the latest disaster prevention weather information, etc.


Life is the most precious thing. In the event of a disaster, remain calm and act without overexerting yourself. Never approach dangerous areas or take risky actions due to overconfidence. Your safety will give peace of mind to those around you. Make your own life your top priority, act quickly and appropriately, and evacuate to a safe place. Survival is the most important thing. Do not overexert yourself, protect your own safety, and cherish your life.
